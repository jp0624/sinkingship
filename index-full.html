  • Annedroids is a science-based action-adventure series for 6-9 year olds. It follows 11-year old genius and kid-scientist Anne, who loves nothing more than doing cool experiments. Anne has invented and built her own amazing androids to help her out. Far more than mere 'robots' (simple machines that can only perform single tasks), Annedroids are thinking, feeling creations with curious, endearing personalities. Thanks to their insatiable curiosity, Anne's experiments always lead to unforeseen complications, hijinks, and misadventures. One day, new kid on the block, Nick, discovers Anne's secret junkyard laboratory. Nick enlists the help of Shania, his slightly eccentric neighbour, and they soon befriend Anne and her mechanical companions. Nick and Shania have uncanny ways of figuring out the real-life solutions to Anne's scientific problems. Needless to say, when two girls, one boy and three androids join forces, excitement and discovery follow.

  • An all-star cast of celebrity comedians share the screen with 18 true-to-life, photo-realistic dinosaurs in the live-action, CGI adventure series Dino Dan, starring Jason Spevack (Fever Pitch, Sunshine Cleaning) as Dan Henderson. Dan - nicknamed 'Dino Dan' by his friends - is an ordinary 10-year old boy with an extraordinary obsession: he eats, sleeps and breathes dinosaurs. A typical day for Dan isn't complete unless he's racing a T-Rex on his school track, finding a Pterodactylus in his attic or getting a baby Brachiosaurus back to its mom. In Dino Dan, you don't need to imagine dinosaurs - because they're everywhere! But while Dino Dan is an expert in all things dinosaurs, he also specializes in getting his willing crew of classmates, teachers and family pulled into his larger-than-life adventures. Personalities including Andrea Martin, Mark McKinney, Kevin McDonald, Sean Cullen, Jayne Eastwood and Jon Dore make guest appearances in the series.

  • The continuing adventures of Dino Dan go to the next level with 16 new prehistoric creatures, a new gang of friends and another Henderson brother who can see dinosaurs. That's right - now Dan's younger brother, Trek, can see the dinosaurs too! At eight years old, Trek has become just as fascinated with dinosaurs as his big brother - and with his own funny way of putting dinos into any situation. With Dan away helping their world-famous paleontologist father, Trek has taken up the mantle of 'paleontologist in training' and has started to complete the experiments in Dan's Dino Field Guide. Trek loves dinosaurs for all the reasons that Dan does but he has not turned into a mini-Dan. Where Dan loves research and experiments, Trek is all about thrills and chills.   • Following in the footsteps of Emmy-nominated Dino Dan, this brand new spin off takes the franchise to a whole new level of dinosaur adventure. 16 new prehistoric creatures, an incredible transforming backpack, an adopted baby dino friend and two sisters who can see the dinosaurs. That's right. A whole new family can see the dinosaurs too! Meet Dana, a spunky 7 year-old "palaeontologist in training" who, like Dan and Trek before her, eats, sleeps and breathes dinos. Emily, her 11 year-old sister, isn't nearly as adventurous and doesn't remotely love dinosaurs. But she's going to learn to love them because, after a chance encounter with Trek and his Dino Field Guide, both sisters can see dinosaurs! The problem is that they don't just see them; they run with them, touch them and ride them too. Dana takes up the unfinished dino experiments given to her by Trek in the Dino Field Guide and uses them as an excuse to get as close as humanly possible to the dinos - something her transforming backpack helps her do - and Emily is always trying to stop her. With two dino-seeing sisters and one pet dinosaur, Dino Dana is an epic new series that promises to double the action and bring the audience closer to the dinosaurs than ever before. Dino Dana continues the award winning tradition of imagining the awesomeness of the past into an adventure in the present.

  • Every kid believes their toys come to life when they're not around and on Playdate, they really do! Mac, Sammy and Priscilla are the beloved stuffed animals of a young brother and sister duo. Each day, these seemingly normal toys sit on the sidelines watching the kids enjoy their playtime adventures. But when the kids are away, Mac, Sammy and Priscilla do their best to recreate the kids' fantasy exploits, becoming racecar drivers, amateur scientists, cowboys and the like. Unfortunately the stuffed toys don't have all the time in the world. Since the kids can never know their toys come to life, Mac, Sammy and Priscilla have to finish their playtime escapades before the kids come back - which is never as easy as it sounds. Luckily, Norman, the family dog, makes the prefect fall guy. You didn't think that kids were the only ones who had playdates did you?

  • Chirp, Squawk and Tweet are best friends who use their imaginations and everyday objects to get them in - and out of - fantastically fun adventures and hilariously sticky predicaments. What would it be like to be a pirate or travel in space, dive underwater on a dolphin or ride a dinosaur? Chirp, Tweet and Squawk may imagine themselves on grand quests through jungles and on spy missions, but ultimately their imaginations get them into trouble. That's where the Mail Squirrel comes in handy. Each day, at the just the right time, the Mail Squirrel drops off a package for Chirp. Everyday items like handheld fans, elastic bands or paper clips become play-savers when Chirp and his friends engineer them into amazing gadgets. By using characters kids relate to and laugh with, coupled with situations they're engaged and entertained by, Chirp, like its namesake magazine, opens up the world to preschoolers, one awesome adventure at a time.

  • Odd Squad is a live-action comedy about two kids, Agent Olive and Agent Otto, who work for a government agency run by kids in suits who are equipped with the world's most advanced and unpredictable gadgetry. Whenever anything strange, unusual or just plain nuts (including giant nuts falling out of the sky) happens, it's Odd Squad's job to put things right again. Odd Squad has a fun, goofy, irreverent style that includes sight gags, one-liners, absurd situations and plenty of slapstick. Embedded in each episode is a math concept aimed at kids in grades 1-2. For example, in one episode, Olive and Otto are called-on to help a pizza delivery lady who keeps doubling herself. In the process of helping her, Olive and Otto also explore and learn what happens when you double a number. Odd Squad is a fun and engaging way for kids to become familiar with, and master, key math concepts and operations.

  • Giver follows teams of 6-12 year-olds who work together with their communities to build and improve local playgrounds. A group of 6 kids must decide on a new theme for their playground - choose what equipment to keep and what to scrap. What does the community want? A sandbox, a garden, a pond or a dog park? On top of all that, they've only got 3 days to do it! Fortunately, the Giver team isn't alone. Our energetic host, Michael, is a handy carpenter. He provides a steady hand and plenty of enthusiasm as the team digs in, drilling, cutting and painting to create a fun new playground for the whole community to enjoy. Artists, builders and community volunteers round out the team as each new project takes shape. With a themes of 'community,' 'construction' and 'consciousness,' Giver sounds the call to action for kids to have fun and get involved! Let's Giver!

  • J.J. Johnson

    Producer / Partner

    J.J. Johnson is a founding partner and the creative force behind Sinking Ship Entertainment. Since the company's inception in 2004, he has created 12 series including international hits Annedroids, Emmy nominated Dino Dan and This is Daniel Cook. J.J. has directed over 150 hours of children's programming and filmed in over 30 countries, he is also the series director on PBS's Odd Squad. J.J. is an Emmy-nominated writer with over 500 episode credits and has been recognized by The Toronto Star and enRoute magazine as one of Canada's top children's television producers. He was listed in Playback magazine as one of their Top 10 to Watch and was named by The Hollywood Reporter as one of the Top 20 under 35 in 2013. J.J. Is also co-chair of the Youth Media Alliance in Canada.

  • Blair Powers

    Producer / Partner

    Blair Powers is a partner and founder of Sinking Ship Entertainment. He heads up the interactive division of the company and oversees the financial, business aspects and funding aspects of all of Sinking Ship's television and digital properties. In addition, Blair has produced award-winning interactive companion websites for many Sinking Ship series including Are we there yet? World Adventure, This is Daniel Cook and Dino Dan's hugely popular and award-nominated interactive suite of online and mobile games and AR apps. Blair began his career after graduating from the School of Radio and Television Arts at Ryerson University in 2002. He is a CMPA member and is currently on the Digital Governance Committee of the Canadian Screen Awards.

  • Matt Bishop

    Producer / Partner

    Matt Bishop is a founding partner of Sinking Ship Entertainment. He leads the animation and visual effect division of Sinking Ship and is an Emmy nominated producer who has producer over 350 half hours of kids television. He is a tech geek who was featured in Post Magazine and Apple.com, along with a live presentation of Sinking Ship's advanced digital workflow for Apple inc. Constantly looking to push technology in as far as possible, Matt lead Sinking Ship in producing the first 4K kids television series production. Matt began his career after graduating from the School of Radio and Television Arts at Ryerson University.

  • Christin Simms

    Head of Development / Executive Producer

    Christin Simms is an award-winning writer and producer who joined Sinking Ship in 2008. In addition to her roles as Executive Producer and Writer on Annedroids, Playdate and Dino Dana, Christin leads the development on all ground-breaking new projects for Sinking Ship. Christin has been nominated for two Emmys, is a recipient of a Canadian Screenwriting Award, was named one of Playback Magazine's Top Ten to Watch and received the Alliance for Children and Television's Emerging Talent Award.

  • Carla de Jong

    Head of Production

    Carla oversees all production across Sinking Ship's exciting slate of projects. Before joining the Ship she held various roles as a producer and broadcaster including Head of Commissioning and Development for ABC Australia and Executive in Charge of Production and Development at Kids' CBC. She created and produced many successful children's series for the BBC in the UK and Network Ten in Australia. Her work has been recognized by the Kidscreen Awards, The Prix Jeunesse Awards, the BANFF World Media Awards, the Logies and AFI Awards in Australia and The United Nations World Environment Day Awards.

  • Georgina López

    Production Manager

    Georgina began at Sinking Ship as Production Coordinator in 2007 and was promoted to Production Manager in 2009. She has worked on nearly EVERY show Sinking Ship has ever done. In her abundant (as if) spare time, she manages her personal projects Indiana (2008) and Ellora (2013).

  • George Lajtai, CSC

    Director of Photography

    George is one of the invisible pillars at Sinking Ship. Since 2004 he has been Director of Photography, helping to tell stories by establishing the visual look of a show through artistic and technical decisions regarding lighting. He has worked on Odd Squad, Annedroids, Dino Dan, Dino Dan: Trek's Adventures,This is Daniel Cook, This is Emily Yeung, Are we there yet?: World Adventure and many more. George's hobby is his work and he feels very lucky for that.

  • Rhonda Olson

    Supervising Producer

    Rhonda's extensive television industry experience is in both production as a writer, story editor and producer and on the broadcasting side as a Production Executive at Corus Entertainment. She has worked in a creative capacity in a broad range of television genres. Select writing and producing credits include: Goosebumps (YTV), The Saddle Club (YTV), Real Kids Real Adventures (YTV), Drop the Beat (CBC), Power Play(CTV), Train 48 (Global), Majumder Manor (W Network) andTo Catch a Killer (OWN).

A self-confessed "gear nerd," Daryl is constantly pushing the limits of technology, coming up with new, more efficient ways to execute effects shots. That's geek-speak for "he likes to push buttons."

True story: David's Ryerson degree (RTA '94) purports his middle name to be Olaf. Foreshadowing!

Prior to Sinking Ship, Laura worked at the Ontario Media Development Corporation, the Canada Media Fund, Alliance Atlantis and SFA Productions.
